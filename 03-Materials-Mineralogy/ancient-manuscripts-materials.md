# Ancient Manuscripts for Studying Materials, Minerals, Metals & Natural Substances

> A curated collection of the most influential ancient and medieval manuscripts on **materials**, **minerals**, **metals**, **stones**, **gems**, **ceramics**, **glass**, **pigments**, **wood**, **construction materials**, and **natural substances** across civilizations.

---

# Overview

Throughout history, civilizations documented materials for numerous disciplines:

- Mineralogy
- Geology
- Metallurgy
- Alchemy
- Gemology
- Mining
- Architecture
- Construction
- Chemistry
- Glassmaking
- Ceramics
- Pigments
- Dyes
- Jewelry
- Coinage
- Medicine

Ancient material science often combined practical craftsmanship with natural philosophy, medicine, cosmology, and, in some traditions, symbolic or alchemical interpretations.

# Building an "Ancient Materials Library"

For a comprehensive study of materials across scientific, engineering, artistic, and historical traditions, consider the following foundational collection:

1. **Kitāb al-Jamāhir fī Maʿrifat al-Jawāhir** — Mineralogy, gemstones, and material properties.
2. **On Stones (Peri Lithon)** — Foundations of mineralogy and geology.
3. **Naturalis Historia** — Encyclopedic coverage of natural materials.
4. **Jabirian Corpus** — Early chemistry and alchemy.
5. **Al-Asrar** — Laboratory techniques and chemical substances.
6. **Meteorologica** — Ancient theories of geological and material formation.
7. **Tiangong Kaiwu** — Engineering, manufacturing, and industrial materials.
8. **Kaogong Ji** — Ancient Chinese craftsmanship and production.
9. **De Diversis Artibus** — Medieval artistic and technical crafts.
10. **De Re Metallica** — Mining, metallurgy, and ore processing.

Together, these manuscripts provide a broad foundation for understanding the natural world of materials—from minerals, metals, and gemstones to wood, glass, ceramics, pigments, and construction materials—through the lenses of science, engineering, craftsmanship, natural philosophy, and the history of technology.
