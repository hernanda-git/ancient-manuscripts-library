# Pañcatantra

**Author:** various
**Era / Date:** c. 3rd c. BCE
**Origin / Tradition:** India
**Language of text:** Sanskrit (English translation)
**Modern discipline(s):** Fables, political ethics, animal tales

## Summary

An ancient Indian frame-tale of five books of animal fables teaching niti (wise conduct and statecraft).

## Why it matters

One of the most translated works in history; the ancestor of Kalila wa-Dimna, Aesop-adjacent fables, and the European beast fable.
