# The Old English Physiologus

**Author:** various
**Era / Date:** 12th–13th c. CE (Latin bestiaries)
**Origin / Tradition:** Medieval Europe
**Language of text:** Latin / English (translation)
**Modern discipline(s):** Medieval folklore, Christian allegory, zoology

## Summary

The Physiologus and its descendant bestiaries — illustrated moralized accounts of real and imaginary beasts, each read as a Christian allegory.

## Why it matters

The most popular natural-history genre of the Middle Ages; shaped the symbolic bestiary tradition (see also Kitab al-Hayawan).
