# Nujūm al-ʿUlūm — "Stars of the Sciences"

**Author:** Muḥammad Qulī ibn Walī, Sultan of Bijapur (r. 1580–1591)
**Date:** Completed 1571 CE (979 AH), Bijapur, Deccan Sultanate
**Language of work:** Persian
**Tradition:** Islamic occult, cosmological & encyclopedic sciences of the late medieval Deccan

## What the work is

*Nujūm al-ʿUlūm* ("Stars of the Sciences") is an illustrated encyclopaedia of the occult and cosmological sciences, composed at the Bijapur court. Its chapters survey the beings and disciplines of the unseen and celestial worlds — including:

- The **jinn**, demons (*shayāṭīn*), and peris (*pari*), with their ranks and natures
- **Astronomy / astrology** (the "stars" of the title) and the seven planets and their angels
- **Talismans** (*ṭilasm*), **magic squares**, **geomancy** (*ʿilm al-raml*), and **oneiromancy** (dream interpretation)
- The **throne of God**, the celestial spheres, and hierarchies of spiritual beings
- Praise of the Prophet Muḥammad and the twelve Imams

It is among the most important illustrated witnesses to the Islamicate "science of the unseen" (*ʿilm al-ghayb*) and pairs naturally with *Shams al-Maʿārif* and the *Ajāʾib* literature already catalogued here.

## Authoritative references (where authentic text/manuscript may be consulted)

- **Primary manuscript:** British Library, MS **Or. 12985** (the only known complete illustrated copy, Bijapur, dated 1571–72). Catalogue: *Catalogue of Persian Manuscripts in the British Library* (Rieu/Bland).
- **Published facsimile (still in copyright):** M.D. Desai (ed./trans.), *Nujūm al-ʿUlūm: Stars of the Sciences*, 2 vols., Baroda, 1981 (bilingual Persian/English edition of the text and plates).
- **Scholarship:** S.C. Welch, *India: Art and Culture 1550–1750* (Met Museum, 1985) — discusses the manuscript's paintings.
